Vanilla Changes
  • All metal or gem storage blocks (for example: Iron Blocks) must be crafted with nine of the materials of choice in a Compressor. Additionally, storage blocks cannot be de-crafted; they must be either smelted (for the original items), or macerated (for 9 Dusts).

  • Mossy Cobblestone can be used in place of Cobblestone in many recipes.
  • Many of the recipes using Iron or Gold ingots now require Plates, Hammers, Wrenches and/or Files for crafting instead. This includes, but not limited to: Tools, Armor, Weighted Pressure Plates, Iron Doors, Minecarts, Hoppers, Buckets and Cauldrons.
  • Wood logs only give two planks instead of the usually four. To get four, you can use a Saw or Plate Cutting Machine.
  • Crafting Paper now only yields two Paper instead of three.
  • Dungeon Chests generally contain much more valuable loot.
  • Monster Spawners are harder to break. This change and last change are made to make dungeons a bit more intresting.
  • Wooden and Stone Tools break much more quickly. However, Flint Tools are good for early-game.
  • (Off by default) Force Adventure Mode, which checks for players in Survival mode and changes them to Adventure mode. In Adventure mode, blocks cannot be broken without the correct tools, however when this option is enabled every player receives a Flint Axe upon their first connection to a server or single-player world.
  • (Off by default) Harder Stone. Pretty self-explanatory, Stone and Cobblestone take longer to break.
  • (Off by default) You cannot craft Enchantment Tables.
  • (Off by default) You cannot craft Ender Chests.
  • (Off by default) You cannot smelt Charcoal from logs.
Industrialcraft 2 Changes
  • The standard IC2 machines are replaced with Automatic IC2 Machines, which in most cases have the same recipes as their counter-parts, except that they can automatically eject their products, can output EU, and use the GregTech Upgrade and Cover systems. Additionally, Automatic Machines will consume the items they are processing at the beginning of the process, rather than at the end.
  • The (Automatic) Electric Furnace requires a High Pressure Steam Furnace instead of an Iron Furnace.
  • Solar Panels are now more expensive requiring Carbon and Silicon Plates, are crafted as covers instead of blocks, and must be combined with a Machine Block (or equivalent) in an Assembling Machine to form their regular IC2 block forms.
  • Tin can no longer be made into Buckets. This is due to exploits that would allow transforming tin to iron.
