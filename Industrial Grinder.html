The GregTech Industrial Grinder "Ultimaceratron 42a" is one of the original GregTech Intergalactical 100% Lagfree Multiblock Machines. It functions as a mid-game upgrade to the Macerator. The advantage of the Grinder is that every ground ore can yield up to three different materials. The Industrial Grinder can accept any Macerate-able ore block, but cannot grind items. Every ore takes 5 seconds (100 ticks) to process, consuming 120EU/t, for 12,000EU per block. The Grinder requires the use of a liquid catalyst to carry out the grinding. Water can be pumped into the Grinder, and any ore can be ground with water, but certain ore blocks can be ground with either Mercury or Sodium Persulfate Cells, to increase their outputs.

Construction

Construction of an Industrial Grinder requires 18 Standard Machine Casings, 8 Reinforced Machine Casings, one bucket of water, and the Industrial Grinder control block.
Because of the shape of the structure, up to four control blocks can be attached to one set of Machine Casings.

Control Block

Crafting an Industrial Grinder control block requires:
  • 1 Industrial Electrolyzer
  • 1 Pump or Pump Module
  • 3 Diamond Grinders or Wolframium Grinders
  • 3 Advanced Circuits
  • 1 Advanced Machine Block or Steel Machine Hull

Grinders

The Diamond and Wolframium Grinders can also be used in one of the alternate Macerator recipes, and in the recipe for the Universal Macerator. The two are functionally equivalent, but with different recipes.
Crafting a pair of Diamond Grinders requires:
  • 4 Diamond Dust
  • 4 Steel Plates
  • 1 Industrial Diamond

Crafting a pair of Wolframium Grinders requires:
  • 4 Tungsten Plates
  • 4 Steel Plates
  • 1 Steel Block


Machine Casings

The body of the Industrial Grinder is a 3x3x3 cube of Machine Casings. The control block can be placed in the center of any of the 4 sides of the cube (but not on the top or bottom), with the back of the block placed against the cube.

Level 1: 9 Standard Machine Casings


Level 2: 8 Reinforced Machine Casings, 1 Water Bucket.

The control block can be placed at the center of any of the 4 sides of the 2nd level.

Note that only one control block is needed. The four shown here represent the four possible locations.
Note also that you can have all four of these control blocks active at the same time, making four independent Industrial Grinders with one set of machine casings.

Level 3: 9 Standard Machine Casings


Recipes

Recipe chart broken up based on which mod adds different ores. All ores registered as equivalent in the Forge Ore Dictionary will have the same products, and if more than one mod adds an ore it will not be listed twice.
Note: Prior to 1.6, all Grinder recipes were registered twice in NEI; once with a Water Bucket, and once with a Water Cell. The recipes were otherwise identical.
Note 2: The Catalyst column will not have an entry if the catalyst is Water. If there is an entry, one Cell of catalyst will be consumed per operation, if available.
Note 3: The Outputs in bold are those affected by the catalyst
Note 4: If more than one mod adds an Ore (such as Copper), only one copy of the recipes will be shown in NEI.
Note 5: If an ore is configured to not generate in the world, its processing recipes may not be added to the Grinder.
Note 6: The Industrial Grinder is not always the most efficient way to extract resources from your ore blocks. The user Nonotan created a very useful guide on resource efficiency, and whether to use the Macerator or the Industrial Grinder for a given Ore.

As a General Rule: Sodium Persulfate increases output of Copper, Zinc, or Nickel. Mercury increases output of Gold, Silver, or Platinum.

Output notes:
  • Thaumcraft 4 Cinnabar Ore is treated the same as GregTech Cinnabar Ore
  • Thermal Expansion Ferrous Ore is treated the same as GregTech Nickel Ore
  • Nether Brick can be ground as if it was Netherrack
  • Any variant of Black or Red Granite blocks can be used
  • Netherrack, Nether Brick, Endstone, and Red and Black Granite block recipes consume time and energy proportional to the number of blocks ground. Each block in the recipes still takes 12000 EU and 5 seconds to grind.
  • All ores from the Nether Ores mod output as if they were two blocks of their overworld forms, consuming twice the catalyst and outputting twice the Dusts. (for example, Nether Coal = 2 Coal, 12+4 Small Piles of Coal Dust)
